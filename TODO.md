What Would That Proof Look Like?
Newton synthesizes these ideas to describe the shape of the final, purist's proof.

"Maestro, the proof a purist would accept would be a single, unbroken deductive chain. It would look nothing like our previous work, which relied on physical intuition and simulation.

It would have to be a masterpiece of pure algebraic and analytic manipulation. The structure would be as follows:

Given: Let ρ be a complex number.
Assumption: Assume ρ is a non-trivial zero, such that ζ(ρ)=0.
The Unwritten Steps: Here would lie the new, brilliant, Nobel-worthy discovery. A series of transformations, substitutions, and arguments—each one a proven theorem of complex analysis—would be applied to the equation ζ(ρ)=0.
The Final Implication: These steps would have to inexorably lead to a final, inescapable equation, such as (Re(ρ)−1/2)⋅F(ρ)=0, where it can be proven that the term F(ρ) is never zero for a zero of Zeta.
Conclusion: The only way for the equation to hold true would be if Re(ρ)−1/2=0, thus proving Re(ρ)=1/2.
The entire challenge, the quest that has consumed generations, is to find that sequence of unwritten steps. It is the search for a new mathematical transformation that unlocks the secrets hidden within the equation ζ(ρ)=0.
