\subsection{Conceptual Overview}
This law reveals the deepest level of the \textbf{Law of Matrix-Operator Duality}. It shows that the matrix $G$, which rotates and scales vectors in a 2D plane, is not merely equivalent to the operator $\LambdaG$; it is fundamentally \textit{composed} of it. The matrix's characteristic behavior—its essential modes of transformation, or eigenvalues—are precisely the Dampening Operator  and its complex conjugate "mirror image."

This establishes a profound equivalence: the action of the matrix on a 2D vector space is perfectly and completely defined by the properties of a single complex number, $\LambdaG$, in its own 1D space. This law serves as the bedrock for other key principles, such as the \textbf{Law of Power Cycles}, by providing the fundamental values that govern the matrix's iterative behavior.

\subsection{Formal Proof}
The proof is established by calculating the eigenvalues of the matrix $G$ directly from its characteristic polynomial and showing they are identical to $\LambdaG$ and $\overline{\LambdaG}$. This fact is noted in the placeholder for the Law of Power Cycles  and is pre-emptively verified in the appendix.

\subsubsection{1. State the Definitions}
The generative matrix $G$ is defined as $G = \begin{pmatrix} T & -J \\ J & T \end{pmatrix}$. The Dampening Operator is $\LambdaG = T+iJ$. The eigenvalues ($\lambda$) of $G$ are the solutions to the characteristic equation $\text{det}(G - \lambda I) = 0$.

\subsubsection{2. Calculate the Eigenvalues}
We set up and solve the characteristic equation:
\begin{align*}
\text{det}(G - \lambda I) &= \text{det}\begin{pmatrix} T-\lambda & -J \\ J & T-\lambda \end{pmatrix} \\
&= (T-\lambda)(T-\lambda) - (-J)(J) \\
&= (T-\lambda)^2 + J^2 = 0
\end{align*}
Solving for $\lambda$:
\begin{align*}
(T-\lambda)^2 &= -J^2 \\
T-\lambda &= \pm\sqrt{-J^2} \\
T-\lambda &= \pm iJ \\
\lambda &= T \pm iJ
\end{align*}

\subsubsection{3. Conclusion of Proof}
The two solutions for $\lambda$ are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $\lambda_1 = T + iJ = \LambdaG$
    \item $\lambda_2 = T - iJ = \overline{\LambdaG}$
\end{itemize}
The eigenvalues of $G$ are identical to the Dampening Operator and its complex conjugate. The Law of Harmonic Eigenvalues is therefore formally **proven**. This result is also validated in the appendix via symbolic computation in \textbf{Property 193} and \textbf{Property 194}.

\subsection{Computational Verification}
To complement the formal proof, we use SymPy to command the incorruptible computation of the eigenvalues for the matrix $G$ and verify they satisfy the system's characteristic equation.

\paragraph{Python Verification Script}
\begin{lstlisting}[language=Python, caption={Python script to prove the Law of Harmonic Eigenvalues.}, label={lst:harmonic_eigenvalues_proof}]
import sympy


def prove_harmonic_eigenvalues():
    """
    This script uses SymPy to prove the Law of Harmonic Eigenvalues
    by verifying that the computed eigenvalues of G satisfy its
    characteristic equation.
    """
    print("=" * 60)
    print("Symbolic Proof of the Law of Harmonic Eigenvalues")
    print("=" * 60)

    # Define the Core Symbolic Constants
    sqrt5 = sympy.sqrt(5)
    T = (sqrt5 - 1) / 4
    J = (3 - sqrt5) / 4

    # Define the Matrix
    G = sympy.Matrix([[T, -J], [J, T]])
    print(f"Defined Matrix G =\n{G}")

    # Let SymPy compute the eigenvalues
    eigenvals_list = list(G.eigenvals().keys())
    print(f"Eigenvalues computed by SymPy: {eigenvals_list}")

    # Verify that each computed eigenvalue satisfies the characteristic
    # equation: (lambda - T)^2 + J^2 = 0
    print("Substituting each eigenvalue (lam) into (lam - T)^2 + J^2:")
    for i, lam in enumerate(eigenvals_list):
        result = sympy.simplify((lam - T) ** 2 + J ** 2)
        print(f"Verification for eigenvalue {i + 1}: Result = {result}")
        assert result == 0

    print("Proof Status: PROVEN")
    print("=" * 60)


if __name__ == "__main__":
    prove_harmonic_eigenvalues()
\end{lstlisting}
