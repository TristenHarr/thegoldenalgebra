\subsubsection*{Conceptual Overview}
The Dampening Operator is the fundamental engine of convergence and stability in the Golden Algebra. It describes the path a system takes as it spirals inward toward a point of equilibrium. Its magnitude being less than one ensures that repeated applications will always reduce the distance to the origin.

\subsection*{Proof of Properties}
We will now prove the central property of the Dampening Operator—that its magnitude is less than one—using both a formal algebraic proof and a computational verification.

\subsubsection*{1. Formal Proof}
The goal is to prove that $|\LambdaG| < 1$, which is the necessary condition for its dampening behavior.

\paragraph{1.1. State the Definitions}
The proof begins with the definitions of the Dampening Operator, $\LambdaG$, and its constituent constants, $T$ and $J$:
$$ \LambdaG = T + iJ, \quad \text{where} \quad T = \frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{4} \quad \text{and} \quad J = \frac{3-\sqrt{5}}{4} $$
The magnitude of a complex number $z = a+ib$ is given by $|z| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$. Thus, the magnitude of $\LambdaG$ is:
$$ |\LambdaG| = \sqrt{T^2 + J^2} $$
To prove that $|\LambdaG| < 1$, we can equivalently prove the more algebraically convenient form $|\LambdaG|^2 < 1^2$, which simplifies to $T^2+J^2 < 1$.

\paragraph{1.2. Calculate the Squared Magnitude}
We first calculate the squares of $T$ and $J$:
\begin{align*}
T^2 &= \left(\frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{4}\right)^2 = \frac{(\sqrt{5})^2 - 2\sqrt{5} + 1}{16} = \frac{5 - 2\sqrt{5} + 1}{16} = \frac{6 - 2\sqrt{5}}{16} \\
J^2 &= \left(\frac{3-\sqrt{5}}{4}\right)^2 = \frac{3^2 - 2(3)\sqrt{5} + (\sqrt{5})^2}{16} = \frac{9 - 6\sqrt{5} + 5}{16} = \frac{14 - 6\sqrt{5}}{16}
\end{align*}

\paragraph{1.3. Substitute and Simplify}
Next, we sum the squared terms to find $|\LambdaG|^2$:
\begin{align*}
|\LambdaG|^2 &= T^2 + J^2 \\
&= \frac{6 - 2\sqrt{5}}{16} + \frac{14 - 6\sqrt{5}}{16} \\
&= \frac{6 - 2\sqrt{5} + 14 - 6\sqrt{5}}{16} \\
&= \frac{20 - 8\sqrt{5}}{16} \\
&= \frac{5 - 2\sqrt{5}}{4}
\end{align*}

\paragraph{1.4. Compare the Result to 1}
Now we must prove that this expression is less than 1.
\begin{align*}
\frac{5 - 2\sqrt{5}}{4} &< 1 \\
5 - 2\sqrt{5} &< 4 \\
1 &< 2\sqrt{5} \\
\frac{1}{2} &< \sqrt{5}
\end{align*}
Squaring both sides of the inequality (which is permissible as both sides are positive) yields:
$$ \frac{1}{4} < 5 $$
This final inequality is unequivocally true. Since all steps in the comparison are reversible, the original inequality holds.

\subsubsection*{2. Computational Verification}
To complement the formal proof, we use a symbolic mathematics library (SymPy in Python) to computationally verify the property.

\subsection*{Conclusion}
Both the formal, step-by-step proof and the symbolic computation demonstrate that $|\LambdaG| < 1$. The defining property of the Dampening Operator is therefore **proven**.

\paragraph{2.1. Python Verification Script}
\begin{lstlisting}[language=Python, caption={Python script to symbolically prove the properties of the Dampening Operator.}, label={lst:dampening_op_proof}]
import sympy

def prove_dampening_operator_properties():
    """
    This script uses the SymPy library for symbolic mathematics to prove the
    properties of the Dampening Operator, specifically that its magnitude < 1.
    """
    print("="*60)
    print("Symbolic Proof of the Dampening Operator Properties")
    print("="*60)

    # Define the Core Symbolic Constants
    sqrt5 = sympy.sqrt(5)
    T = (sqrt5 - 1) / 4
    J = (3 - sqrt5) / 4
    Lambda_G1 = T + sympy.I * J

    print(f"Defined T = {T}")
    print(f"Defined J = {J}")
    print(f"Defined Lambda_G1 = T + iJ = {Lambda_G1}\\n")

    # --- Prove the Magnitude Property: |Lambda_G1| < 1 ---
    print("\\n--- Verifying the Magnitude Property: |Lambda_G1| < 1 ---")

    # To avoid dealing with square roots, we prove that |Lambda_G1|^2 < 1
    # |Lambda_G1|^2 = T^2 + J^2
    mag_sq = sympy.simplify(T**2 + J**2)
    print(f"Squared Magnitude |Lambda_G1|^2 = T^2 + J^2 = {mag_sq}")

    # The simplify function can directly evaluate the inequality
    is_less_than_one = sympy.simplify(mag_sq < 1)

    print(f"Is the squared magnitude < 1? {is_less_than_one}")
    assert is_less_than_one

    # For clarity, we can also show the numerical evaluation
    numerical_mag_sq = mag_sq.evalf()
    print(f"Numerical value of squared magnitude: {numerical_mag_sq}")
    print(f"Numerical value of magnitude: {sympy.sqrt(numerical_mag_sq).evalf()}")

    print("="*60)
    print("Conclusion: The property |Lambda_G1| < 1 is computationally verified.")
    print("="*60)


if __name__ == '__main__':
    prove_dampening_operator_properties()
\end{lstlisting}
