\subsubsection*{Conceptual Overview}
This law provides the 'destination' for any system governed by the Dampening Operator.  For any contracting spiral, this formula calculates the exact point of convergence.  This is the definition of a stable equilibrium point within the framework, representing the geometric sum of all the vectors in the spiral's infinite path.

\subsubsection*{Formal Proof}
The proof follows directly from the standard formula for the sum of an infinite geometric series.  The law is proven by showing that a spiral generated by $\LambdaG$ is such a series and that it meets the condition for convergence.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item A spiral beginning at a point $p_0$ and iterating with the Dampening Operator $\LambdaG$ generates a sequence of points $p_0, p_1, p_2, \dots$, where each point is a vector from the origin. The sequence is defined by $p_k = p_0 \cdot (\LambdaG)^k$.

    \item The final convergence point, $\Sigma_G$, is the sum of this infinite sequence of vectors:
    $$ \Sigma_G = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} p_k = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} p_0 (\LambdaG)^k $$

    \item This is a standard geometric series with first term $a = p_0$ and common ratio $r = \LambdaG$.

    \item The formula for the sum of an infinite geometric series is $S = \frac{a}{1-r}$. A series converges and this formula is valid if and only if the magnitude of the common ratio is less than one, i.e., $|r| < 1$.

    \item In the chapter on the Dampening Operator, we formally proved that $|\LambdaG| < 1$.  Therefore, the series converges.

    \item Substituting our first term and common ratio into the sum formula yields the convergence point:
    $$ \Sigma_G = \frac{p_0}{1 - \LambdaG} $$
\end{enumerate}
The law is therefore formally **proven**.

\subsubsection*{Computational Verification}
The following Python script uses SymPy's symbolic summation capabilities to prove that the infinite sum of the spiral's points is algebraically identical to the formula provided by the law. The proof's validity rests on applying the `refine` function with the assumption that $|\LambdaG|<1$, a condition which was proven in the chapter on the Dampening Operator. 

\paragraph{Python Verification Script}
\begin{lstlisting}[language=Python, caption={Symbolic script to prove the Law of Invariant Geometric Sum.}, label={lst:geom_sum_symbolic_proof}]
import sympy


def prove_invariant_geometric_sum_symbolically():
    """
    Symbolically proves the Law of Invariant Geometric Sum using SymPy,
    applying the necessary convergence assumption.
    """
    print("=" * 60)
    print("Symbolic Proof of the Law of Invariant Geometric Sum")
    print("=" * 60)

    # Define symbolic constants and variables
    T, J, p0 = sympy.symbols('T J p0', real=True)
    k = sympy.Symbol('k', integer=True, nonnegative=True)

    # Define the operator
    Lambda_G1 = T + sympy.I * J

    # 1. State the formula for the sum
    formula_sum = p0 / (1 - Lambda_G1)

    # 2. State the necessary condition for convergence, which we have
    # already proven in the 'Dampening Operator' section.
    convergence_condition = sympy.Abs(Lambda_G1) < 1

    # 3. Calculate the sum using SymPy's symbolic summation
    series_sum = sympy.summation(p0 * (Lambda_G1 ** k), (k, 0, sympy.oo))

    # 4. Refine the summation under the convergence condition
    refined_sum = sympy.refine(series_sum, convergence_condition)

    print(f"Sum from Formula: {formula_sum}")
    print(f"Sum from Direct Summation (refined): {refined_sum}")

    # 5. Verify the refined sum equals the formula
    assert sympy.simplify(formula_sum - refined_sum) == 0

    print("Proof Status: PROVEN")
    print("=" * 60)
    print("Conclusion: SymPy's direct summation of the infinite series,")
    print("when refined with the proven convergence condition,")
    print("yields the same expression as the law's formula.")
    print("=" * 60)


if __name__ == '__main__':
    prove_invariant_geometric_sum_symbolically()
    
\end{lstlisting}
