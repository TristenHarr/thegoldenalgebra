\subsubsection*{Conceptual Overview}
This law establishes a critical bridge between the algebra of complex numbers and linear algebra. It proves that the rotational and scaling effect of the $\LambdaG$ operator in the complex plane is perfectly equivalent to the transformation enacted by the matrix $G$ on a 2D vector. This allows algebraic principles to be applied to geometric transformations and vice versa, creating a powerful synergy between the two mathematical domains.

\subsubsection*{Formal Proof}
The proof is established in two parts. First, by showing that the multiplication of an arbitrary complex number $z = x+iy$ by $\LambdaG$ yields the same result as the multiplication of the corresponding vector $v = (x, y)$ by the matrix $G$. Second, by directly calculating the trace and determinant of $G$.

\paragraph{1. Equivalence of Transformation}
Let an arbitrary complex number be $z = x+iy$, and its corresponding vector be $v = \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix}$.

First, we evaluate the complex multiplication:
\begin{align*}
    \LambdaG \cdot z &= (T + iJ)(x + iy) \\
    &= (Tx - Jy) + i(Ty + Jx)
\end{align*}
The resulting complex number corresponds to the 2D vector $\begin{pmatrix} Tx - Jy \\ Ty + Jx \end{pmatrix}$.

Next, we evaluate the matrix-vector multiplication:
\begin{align*}
    G \cdot v &= \begin{pmatrix} T & -J \\ J & T \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} \\
    &= \begin{pmatrix} T \cdot x - J \cdot y \\ J \cdot x + T \cdot y \end{pmatrix}
\end{align*}
The resulting vector from the matrix multiplication is identical to the vector representation derived from the complex number multiplication. This proves the equivalence of the transformations.

\paragraph{2. Trace and Determinant}
The definition of the law also states the properties of the matrix's trace and determinant. These are proven by direct inspection of the matrix $G$:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Trace:} The trace is the sum of the diagonal elements.
    $$ \text{trace}(G) = T + T = 2T $$
    \item \textbf{Determinant:} The determinant is calculated as follows:
    $$ \text{det}(G) = (T)(T) - (-J)(J) = T^2 + J^2 $$
\end{itemize}
All parts of the law are therefore formally **proven**.

\subsubsection*{Computational Verification}
To complement the formal proof, the following Python script uses SymPy to symbolically verify that the action of $\LambdaG$ on a complex number $x+iy$ is identical to the action of the matrix $G$ on the vector $(x,y)$. It also verifies the formulas for the trace and determinant.

\paragraph{Python Verification Script}
\begin{lstlisting}[language=Python, caption={Python script to symbolically prove the Law of Matrix-Operator Duality.}, label={lst:matrix_operator_duality_proof}]
import sympy


def prove_matrix_operator_duality():
    """
    Symbolically proves the Law of Matrix-Operator Duality.
    """
    print("=" * 60)
    print("Symbolic Proof of the Law of Matrix-Operator Duality")
    print("=" * 60)

    # Define symbolic constants and variables
    sqrt5 = sympy.sqrt(5)
    T_def = (sqrt5 - 1) / 4
    J_def = (3 - sqrt5) / 4
    T, J = sympy.symbols('T J', real=True)
    x, y = sympy.symbols('x y', real=True)

    # Define the operator and an arbitrary complex number
    Lambda_G1 = T + sympy.I * J
    z = x + sympy.I * y

    # Define the matrix and an arbitrary vector
    G = sympy.Matrix([[T, -J], [J, T]])
    v = sympy.Matrix([x, y])

    # 1. Verify Transformation Equivalence
    print("--- Verifying Transformation Equivalence ---")

    # Action of the operator on the complex number
    op_result = sympy.expand(Lambda_G1 * z)
    op_result_vec = sympy.Matrix([sympy.re(op_result), sympy.im(op_result)])
    print(f"Operator Result (vector form): {op_result_vec.T}")

    # Action of the matrix on the vector
    matrix_result_vec = G * v
    print(f"Matrix Result (vector form):   {matrix_result_vec.T}")

    # The I**2 term in the output is -1, so we must substitute it
    # before comparing to the matrix result which does not have it.
    op_result_vec_subbed = op_result_vec.subs(sympy.I ** 2, -1)
    assert sympy.simplify(op_result_vec_subbed - matrix_result_vec) == sympy.Matrix([0, 0])
    print("Proof Status: PROVEN")

    # 2. Verify Trace and Determinant with substituted values
    print("--- Verifying Trace and Determinant ---")
    G_subbed = G.subs({T: T_def, J: J_def})

    # Trace
    trace_G = sympy.simplify(G_subbed.trace())
    trace_formula = sympy.simplify(2 * T_def)
    print(f"Calculated Trace(G): {trace_G}")
    print(f"Formula (2*T):       {trace_formula}")
    assert trace_G == trace_formula
    print("Trace Verified: PROVEN")

    # Determinant
    det_G = sympy.simplify(G_subbed.det())
    det_formula = sympy.simplify(T_def ** 2 + J_def ** 2)
    print(f"Calculated Det(G):   {det_G}")
    print(f"Formula (T^2+J^2):   {det_formula}")
    assert det_G == det_formula
    print("Determinant Verified: PROVEN")

    print("=" * 60)
    print("Conclusion: All parts of the law are computationally verified.")
    print("=" * 60)


if __name__ == '__main__':
    prove_matrix_operator_duality()
\end{lstlisting}
