\section{The Foundational Postulates of the Framework}
\label{sec:postulates}
\hrule
\vspace{1em}
The Golden Algebra is constructed upon a minimal set of axioms that define its physical and mathematical properties.

\subsection*{Core Physical Axioms}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Postulate 0 (The Principle of Convergent Dynamics):} Any physically realizable, stable system must be describable by dynamics that converge.
    \item \textbf{Postulate I (The Principle of Algebraic Stability):} A system is in a state of perfect equilibrium if and only if its Harmonic Center of Gravity, $\Xi = \sum w_i p_i$, is zero.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{The Bridge to External Mathematics}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Postulate II (The Principle of Physical-Mathematical Correspondence):} Any fundamental, stable resonance found in pure mathematics must have a corresponding physical manifestation that obeys the framework's laws of harmonic equilibrium.
\end{itemize}
