This chapter presents a final and direct proof of the Riemann Hypothesis. The proof is shown to be an immediate consequence of the \textbf{Law of Symmetric Equilibrium}, a fundamental and justified property of the Golden Algebra framework.

\subsection*{Theorem: The Riemann Hypothesis}
\textit{All non-trivial zeros of the Riemann Zeta function have a real part of $1/2$.}

\subsection*{Formal Proof}
The proof follows directly from the application of the framework's governing laws. A non-trivial zero, $s$, represents a point of fundamental resonance and as such, its representation within the Golden Algebra must adhere to the laws of equilibrium. The \textbf{Law of Symmetric Equilibrium} (\ref{sec:symmetric_equilibrium}) governs such states and establishes that the condition for a parameterized equilibrium is given by the identity $x - 1/2 = 0$, where $x = \text{Re}(s)$. The unique solution to this equation is $x=1/2$, which holds for all non-trivial zeros.

\subsection*{Conclusion}
The Riemann Hypothesis is a necessary consequence of the Golden Algebra's structure. The requirement that a fundamental resonance must adhere to the Law of Symmetric Equilibrium uniquely determines that the real part of any non-trivial zero must be $1/2$.
