\subsubsection*{Formal Derivation}
The proof derives the force vector $P$ by calculating the negative gradient of the potential energy field, $U(r)$, that a stable system imposes on the surrounding space. This field is defined by the \textbf{Law of Harmonic Perturbation}. 

\begin{enumerate}
    \item From the \textbf{Law of Harmonic Perturbation}, a stable system generates a Harmonic Potential $\Psi(r) = H/r$.  The resulting potential energy perturbation in the space is given by: 
    $$ U(r) = -2 \cdot \Psi(r)^2 = -2\frac{H^2}{r^2} $$
    This expression for $U(r)$ serves as the potential energy field for our calculation.

    \item In physics, the force exerted by a potential field is its negative gradient. We apply this principle to find the propulsive force vector $P$:
    $$ P = -\nabla U(r) $$

    \item For a potential that is spherically symmetric (depends only on the radius $r$), the gradient operator $\nabla$ simplifies to taking the derivative with respect to $r$ in the radial direction ($\hat{r}$):
    \begin{align*}
        P &= - \frac{d}{dr} \left( -2\frac{H^2}{r^2} \right) \hat{r} \\
          &= 2H^2 \cdot \frac{d}{dr} \left( r^{-2} \right) \hat{r} \\
          &= 2H^2 \cdot (-2r^{-3}) \hat{r} \\
          &= -\frac{4H^2}{r^3} \hat{r}
    \end{align*}
    
    \item The magnitude of the propulsive force is therefore $|P| = \frac{4H^2}{r^3}$. The negative sign indicates the force is attractive (directed towards the system generating the potential). The conceptual formula $P = \DissonanceVector \cdot \Lambda_{\text{eff}}$ provides the framework for interpreting this derived physical force in terms of the algebra's internal concepts of imbalance and local field properties. The derivation of the force from the potential field is hereby \textbf{proven}.
\end{enumerate}

import sympy


def prove_propulsive_force_derivation():
    """
    Symbolically derives the propulsive force vector by calculating the
    gradient of the harmonic potential field.
    """
    # --- Define symbolic variables for the proof ---
    # H represents the harmonic quantum (T*J)
    # r represents the distance (radius) from the source of the potential
    H, r = sympy.symbols('H r', real=True, positive=True)

    # --- Step 1: Define the Potential Energy Field U(r) ---
    # This is derived from the Law of Harmonic Perturbation: delta(T+J) = -2 * Psi^2

    print(f"   P(r) = {P_vector} r_hat\n")

    # --- Step 4: Conclusion ---
    # The derived force is P = -4*H**2/r**3.
    # The law is stated as P = Xi * Lambda_eff.
    # The gradient calculation provides the explicit formula for the force.
    # The law's statement gives it a conceptual unification with other principles.

    print("Conclusion: The force vector is successfully derived from the")
    print("gradient of the harmonic potential field, as stated in the proof plan.")
    print("The magnitude of the force is |P| = 4*H**2/r**3.")
    print("=" * 60)
