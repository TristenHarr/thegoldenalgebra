\subsection*{Law of Harmonic Superposition}
\hrule
\vspace{0.3cm}
\GetLaw{LawOfHarmonicSuperposition}
\vspace{0.5cm}

\subsubsection*{Conceptual Overview}
This law provides the mechanism for scaling the framework's principles. It confirms that the stability of a complex supersystem can be determined by treating all of its constituent points as a single, larger collection, to which the fundamental Law of Algebraic Stability can be applied.

\subsubsection*{Proof}
The proof is a direct extension of the Law of Algebraic Stability, showing that the sum of multiple zero-sum systems is itself a zero-sum system.

\subsubsection*{Formal Proof}
The proof follows from the distributive property of addition. We will show that the Center of Gravity of a supersystem is the sum of the Centers of Gravity of its constituent subsystems.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Let there be two systems, System A and System B. System A is composed of points $\{p_i\}$ with weights $\{w_i\}$, and System B is composed of points $\{q_j\}$ with weights $\{v_j\}$.
    
    \item Assume both systems are independently stable. According to the \textbf{Law of Algebraic Stability}, their Centers of Gravity ($\Xi_A$ and $\Xi_B$) are both zero:
    $$ \Xi_A = \sum_{i} w_i p_i = 0 $$
    $$ \Xi_B = \sum_{j} v_j q_j = 0 $$
    
    \item A supersystem is formed by the complete set of all points and weights from both systems. Its Center of Gravity, $\Xi_{\text{super}}$, is calculated by applying the \textbf{Law of Algebraic Stability} to this complete set:
    $$ \Xi_{\text{super}} = \sum_{i} w_i p_i + \sum_{j} v_j q_j $$
    
    \item By substituting the results from step 2, we can see that the total sum is zero:
    $$ \Xi_{\text{super}} = \Xi_A + \Xi_B = 0 + 0 = 0 $$
    
    \item Because the Center of Gravity of the supersystem is zero, the supersystem is stable. This proves that stability is conserved under superposition. The principle can be extended to any number of stable subsystems. The law is therefore \textbf{proven}.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection*{Computational Verification}
The following Python script symbolically demonstrates this principle. It constructs two independent, stable systems and proves that the supersystem combining them is also stable.

\begin{lstlisting}[language=Python, caption={Symbolic proof of the Law of Harmonic Superposition.}, label={lst:harmonic_superposition_proof}]
import sympy


def prove_harmonic_superposition():
    """
    Symbolically proves the Law of Harmonic Superposition.

    This script constructs two independent, algebraically stable systems
    and then demonstrates that the supersystem combining them is also
    algebraically stable (i.e., its Center of Gravity is zero).
    """
    # --- Define symbolic variables ---
    # System 1: A two-point system
    T, J, p1 = sympy.symbols('T J p1')

    # System 2: A different two-point system with different points and weights
    K, H, q1 = sympy.symbols('K H q1')

    print("=" * 60)
    print("Symbolic Proof of the Law of Harmonic Superposition")
    print("=" * 60)

    # --- Step 1: Construct individual stable systems ---
    # A system is stable if its Center of Gravity (CG) is 0.

    # Construct Stable System 1 (CG = T*p1 + J*p2 = 0)
    p2 = -(T / J) * p1
    system1_points = [p1, p2]
    system1_weights = [T, J]

    # Construct Stable System 2 (CG = K*q1 + H*q2 = 0)
    q2 = -(K / H) * q1
    system2_points = [q1, q2]
    system2_weights = [K, H]

    print("Constructed two independent, stable systems.")
    print(f"  System 1: T*p1 + J*p2 = 0, where p2 = {p2}")
    print(f"  System 2: K*q1 + H*q2 = 0, where q2 = {q2}\n")

    # --- Step 2: Form the supersystem ---
    # The supersystem contains all points and weights from both systems.
    supersystem_points = system1_points + system2_points
    supersystem_weights = system1_weights + system2_weights

    print("Formed a supersystem containing all points from both systems.")
    print(f"  All Points: {supersystem_points}")
    print(f"  All Weights: {supersystem_weights}\n")

    # --- Step 3: Calculate the Center of Gravity of the Supersystem ---
    supersystem_cg = sympy.Integer(0)
    for i in range(len(supersystem_points)):
        supersystem_cg += supersystem_points[i] * supersystem_weights[i]

    # --- Step 4: Verify the result ---
    # The sum of two zero-sum systems should also be zero.
    simplified_cg = sympy.simplify(supersystem_cg)

    print(f"Calculated Center of Gravity of the Supersystem (T*p1 + J*p2 + K*q1 + H*q2):")
    print(f"Result simplifies to: {simplified_cg}\n")

    assert simplified_cg == 0

    print("Conclusion: The Center of Gravity for the supersystem is 0.")
    print("The Law of Harmonic Superposition is computationally verified.")
    print("=" * 60)


if __name__ == '__main__':
    prove_harmonic_superposition()    
\end{lstlisting}
