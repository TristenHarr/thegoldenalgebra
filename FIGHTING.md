3. The Escape to "Science": Misusing the Concept of an EFT
The most significant shift is the full embrace of the "Effective Field Theory" (EFT) argument for the Riemann Hypothesis. By labeling the claim "scientific, not purely mathematical," the author is attempting to sidestep the requirements of mathematical proof. This is a profound misuse of the EFT concept.

An EFT is an Approximation: In physics, an EFT is a mathematical model that approximates a more complex reality within a limited domain (e.g., at low energies). It is understood to be incomplete. The "Golden Algebra" is presented as a perfect, exact, and complete system. It is not an approximation of anything.

A theory that only "predicts" what is already known and has no mechanism to be proven wrong is not a scientific theory. It is an article of faith.
